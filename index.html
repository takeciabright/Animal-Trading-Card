Giant Phantom Jellyfish

NO. 110 Venomous Animal
The Phantom Jelly is able to control its own body density to travel through various depths of the ocean.
  • Scientific Name: Stygiomedusa Gigantea
  • Average Length: 33 feet
  • Average Lifespan: Unknown
  • Habitat: Bathypelagic (Midnight Zone)

The Phantom Jelly, a rare species of animal, is only seen once every year. Its first appearance was captured by a remote control submersible in the ocean's midnight zone. Phantom has an umbrella-shaped bell head that can grow up to 3.3 feet tall. Its pliant tissue allows the jelly to grow 4 to 5 times its size, reaching past to 33 feet. This giant jelly is presumably a facultative planktivore, meaning it only consumes plankton and small fish.

The Immortal Jelly, a transparent bell-shaped animal with a bright red center, possesses an amazing cellular mechanism called transdifferentiation. This enables them to revert back to their "baby stage" in response to physical damage or even starvation. First spotted in the 1990s, scientists began documenting their genetics and discovered that they were all genetically identical, even as their cells were replaced.
